Case Study: The Pizza Puzzle

Topic: Deceptive Advertising

Involved Parties:

George Hansen, General Manager of Augusta Marigold Inn, a Subsidiary of Hospitality Enterprises
Sharon Coombs, Food Services Manager, Augusta Marigold Inn
George Hansen is General Manager for the Marigold Inn in Augusta, Georgia. Sharon Coombs is Restaurant and Food Services Manager for the Inn. She reports to George. Two years ago, Sharon noticed a decline in room service business, the highest margin portion of her operation. This decline coincided with an increase in the national sales of pizza delivery and carryout firms as well as an increase in the number of empty pizza boxes from these firms being left in guest rooms in the Inn. Her immediate response was to install a pizza oven in the kitchen and offer room service pizza to guests. The effort met with modest success, though it was well below her expectations. Questionnaires completed by departing guests revealed a problem of product quality.

Focusing on this problem, Sharon improved the Inn’s pizza until blind taste tests judged it at least equal in quality to the products of the two major pizza delivery competitors in Augusta. Sales did not improve, convincing Sharon that the problem was a perceived mismatch between the hotel’s image and guests’ expectations of pizza makers. Guests simply did not seem to believe that the traditional steak and seafood restaurant at the Inn could make a high-quality, authentic pizza. Based on this conclusion, Sharon presented the following proposal to George:

Sales of room service pizza are stagnant due to guests’ misperception that our product is lower in quality than that of competitors. This misperception is based on the belief that until we disassociate our pizza from the Marigold Inn name. Therefore, to capture more room service pizza business, we should create a ‘Napoli Pizza’ image for our guest room delivery service by:

Preparing ‘Napoli Pizza’ brochures for each guest room, complete with a phone number with a prefix different from that of Marigold Inn.
The number will reach a special phone in room service, which will be answered, "Napoli Pizza, authentic Italian pizza from old, family recipes.”
Using special ‘Napoli Pizza’ boxes for delivering room service pizza to guests.
Issuing ‘Napoli Pizza’ hats and jackets to room service personnel for use in pizza delivery. Room service waiters and waitresses will wear these garments to deliver pizza. They will change to their regular uniforms for other deliveries.
How should George respond to this proposal?

I. はじめに (Introduction) (約1/2ページ)
Augusta Marigold Innは伝統的なステーキ＆シーフードレストランが有名なホテルである。フードサービスマネージャーのシャロンはルームサービスの落ち込みが宅配ピザ業界の成長と重なっていることに気づき、ピザの品質向上を図る。しかし、顧客の誤解が売上に影響していると判断し、「ナポリ・ピザ」ブランドの創設を提案。本ペーパーでは、支配人のジョージがこの提案にどう対応すべきかを検討する。倫理的考察のポイントはビジネスの誠実さ、透明性、そして顧客への責任である。

II. 提案の詳細とビジネス上の意図 (Proposal Details and Business Intent) (約1/2ページ)
シャロンはホテルの名前を使ったルームサービスが売り上げを減少させていると判断し、異なるブランドを創設しようとしている。ルームサービスとは異なる電話番号や制服を使うことで、ピザを顧客にアピールする戦略を提案。この提案が成功すれば、短期的な利益として売上増加や顧客の誤解解消が見込まれる。一方、長期的な影響として、ホテルとピザ両方のブランドイメージ低下や顧客との信頼関係の損失が懸念される。その理由は、新しいブランドは実質的にホテルが提供しているにも関わらず、顧客には虚偽の印象を与える可能性があるためである。

III. 倫理的課題の分析 (Ethical Issues Analysis) (約1ページ)
虚偽表示と透明性の欠如:
ビジネス倫理の範囲は時代とともに拡大しており、現代ではサプライチェーンも含めた企業の透明性が求められている。Gordon (2023) は "The importance of an ethical supply chain lies in its impact on a company's reputation, financial performance, and broader societal impacts." と述べており、社会への影響までを考慮した倫理観が必要である。特に食品業界では、原材料や製造過程の透明性が重要視されており、消費者に対する誠実さが求められている。そのため、ピザの製造元を隠すことは、消費者に対する欺瞞にあたる。顧客は商品の品質や提供元に対して正確な情報を求めており、虚偽のブランドイメージは顧客の信頼を損なう可能性がある。また、競合他社との公平な競争環境を損ねる可能性もあるため、企業としての倫理的責任を果たすためには、誠実さと透明性を重視する必要がある。以上の理由から、シャロンの案は持続可能なサプライチェーンで成り立つとは考えられない。
さらに、シャロンの提案からはそれ以外の部分にも疑惑をかけるべき点が見受けられる。例えば、競合他社と同等のクオリティを目指したピザの説明が "authentic Italian pizza from old, family recipes" となっている点は検証が必要だ。もしこの表現が事実に基づいておらず誇大広告である場合、消費者に対する欺瞞が生じる可能性がある。Johns (2023) はその危険性をこう説明する。"Exaggerated claims may lead to short-term sales, but they can also result in disappointed customers and negative reviews." 直接的に嘘をついていないとしても、FTCは "False implied claims are illegal." と断罪している。 企業は発信する言葉に責任を持ち、消費者の誤解を招かない表現であることを重視すべきである。

競合他社への影響:
誇大広告やサプライチェーンの偽装は不当な競争行為に該当し、市場の公正性を損ねる行為である。仮にシャロンの計画が成功した場合、他社も同様の手法を取る可能性があり、結果的に社会全体に悪影響を及ぼす可能性がある。その結果は消費者の信頼喪失につながり、ひいては業界全体の信頼を損なう可能性がある。企業倫理の欠落は時として人の命を奪う。バングラデシュでは、工場の安全基準を無視した結果、1100人以上の労働者が死亡するという悲劇が発生した。
Ethics Unwrapped は "firms have an ethical obligation to provide reasonable safety to workers" と主張する。 (Collapse at Rana Plaza, n/d) Islam & Tahasin (2023) はこの事故は倫理よりも利益を優先し続けた業界の責任であり、Bangladesh全体が企業倫理の維持に苦戦していることが背景にあると指摘している。彼らはIn addition to considering how their actions would affect shareholders, businesses also need to consider how their decisions will influence other stakeholders, such as consumers, employees, communities, and the environment.と述べており、企業はステークホルダーだけでなく、社会全体に責任を負うべきだと説いている。シャロンの提案は業界全体にとって安全なものだとはいえない。

従業員への影響:
シャロンの提案はバングラデシュの事故に比べれば小さい倫理違反かもしれないが、従業員の評判を下げる可能性がある以上、従業員にとって安全な計画とは言い難い。従業員は企業の代表として行動し、企業の倫理観を体現する存在である。シャロンは従業員とその家族らのレピュテーションを守るべきであるにも関わらず、その提案は彼らを危険にさらすものだ。さらに非倫理的な業務指示は従業員のモチベーションを下げ、ピザの味や接客のクオリティを低下させるだろう。Zhao & Qu (2022) の興味深い研究結果は、会社のために倫理に反することをする従業員は、個人的にも倫理的な行動をしなくなる傾向があるというものだった。そのような従業員を抱えることはシャロンにとっても企業にとってもリスクである。

IV. 代替案の検討 (Alternative Solutions) (約1/2ページ)
シャロンは透明性を維持した改善案を提示するべきである。例えばピザの開発の際に行ったブラインドテストを顧客に公開し、競合他社と同等の味を実現したことを発信するといった戦略が考えられる。顧客がルームサービスのクオリティを誤解しているとシャロンは考えている。誤解を解くには、必要十分な情報で顧客を教育する必要があるというのがJohns の主張です。商品に自信があるのなら、誇大広告をせずに素直に他社と同等の味を実現しました！といえばいいでしょう。また、顧客からのフィードバックを積極的に取り入れ、その足跡を公開することで顧客との信頼関係を構築できると考えられます。これらも情報を公開し、透明性を高めることで、消費者に対する誠実さを示すことができます。
その他のアプローチとしては、そもそも調理場が宅配ピザよりも近いのだから、提供スピードをアピールすることもできます。さらに、ルームサービスにもレストランのブランドを連携させることで、顧客により一層の信頼を得るといった戦略も考えられます。

ビジネスにおける倫理の重要性を改めて強調し、将来の意思決定に活かすべき教訓を示す。

ジョージはシャロンの努力や分析、提案のいい点を認めつつも、倫理的な観点から代替案を採用することを推奨するべきです。そして企業の信頼性は消費者との信頼関係によって築かれるものであり、虚偽のブランドイメージは企業の信頼を損なう可能性があることを彼女に伝える必要があります。レストランとフードサービスのマネージャーであるシャロンには高いビジネス倫理を持ち、透明性を重視することが強く求められます。今回の事案を通じて、総支配人のジョージに求められているのは、企業全体の倫理的な意識を向上させることです。昨今の企業倫理は複雑であり、日々その形を変えています。一年前には許されたキャッチコピーが、今日には人種差別だとして非難されることもあります。ジョージはシャロンや従業員と共に定期的な倫理研修の受講することを検討するべきです。これにより、企業の倫理的な意識を高め、将来の意思決定に活かすことができるでしょう。

Conclusion
企業倫理はビジネスの持続可能性に直結する重要な要素である。シャロンの提案は利益を重視した観点では魅力的だが、企業にとって大きなリスクがある。このリスクに気づく鍵は倫理的観点だ。倫理的観点が不足している企業は短期的な利益を追求した結果、長期的な利益を失う。つまり、長期的な利益を企業にもたらすものは倫理的観点とそれを実行する知識や経験であるということだ。倫理と行動のバランスは一朝一夕では身につかない。日々の意思決定において倫理的な観点を意識し続けることで、企業も従業員も倫理観を成長させることができる。ジョージとシャロンの二人がこの事案を通じて倫理的な観点を再確認しその重要性を学習することができれば、彼らの企業はより持続可能なビジネスを築くことができるだろう。

Abstract
 In this paper, George, General Manager of the Augusta Marigold Inn, will examine from an ethical perspective how he should respond to Food Service Manager Sharon's proposal for a “Napoli Pizza” brand. Through the details of the proposal and its business intent, analysis of the ethical issues, consideration of alternatives, and George's response and conclusions, the paper will emphasize the importance of business ethics and provide a road map for building a sustainable business.


I. Introduction

The Augusta Marigold Inn is a hotel renowned for its traditional steak and seafood restaurant. Sharon, the food service manager, noticed a decline in room service coinciding with the growth of the pizza delivery industry and sought to improve the quality of their pizza offerings. However, believing customer misconceptions were impacting sales, she proposed creating a new "Napoli Pizza" brand. This paper examines how George, the general manager, should respond to this proposal. The key ethical considerations are business integrity, transparency, and responsibility to the customer.

II. Proposal Details and Business Intent

Sharon believes that using the hotel's name for room service is hindering pizza sales and proposes creating a distinct brand. By using a separate phone number and uniforms, she aims to better market the pizza to customers. If successful, this proposal promises short-term benefits such as increased sales and addressing customer misconceptions. However, potential long-term consequences include damage to both the hotel and the pizza brand image, and a loss of customer trust. This is because the new brand, while effectively offered by the hotel, could create a misleading impression for customers.

III. Ethical Issues Analysis

Misrepresentation and Lack of Transparency

Business ethics has evolved to encompass greater transparency, including within supply chains. Gordon (2023) states, "The importance of an ethical supply chain lies in its impact on a company's reputation, financial performance, and broader societal impacts," highlighting the need for ethical considerations that extend to societal impacts. Transparency regarding ingredients and manufacturing processes is particularly crucial in the food industry, demanding honesty with consumers. Concealing the pizza's origin would constitute deception. Customers expect accurate information about product quality and source, and a false brand image could erode their trust. It could also create an unfair competitive environment. Therefore, upholding ethical responsibility requires prioritizing honesty and transparency. For these reasons, Sharon's proposal is unlikely to be based on a sustainable supply chain.
Furthermore, Sharon's proposal raises other concerns. For instance, the pizza's description as "authentic Italian pizza from old, family recipes," despite aiming for quality comparable to competitors, requires verification. If this statement is untrue or constitutes exaggerated advertising, it could mislead consumers. Johns (2023) explains the danger: "Exaggerated claims may lead to short-term sales, but they can also result in disappointed customers and negative reviews." Even if not explicitly false, the FTC condemns "False implied claims are illegal." (Ensor, 2024) Businesses must be responsible for their messaging and ensure they do not mislead consumers.

Impact on Competitors

 Exaggerated advertising and supply chain misrepresentation constitute unfair competition, undermining market fairness. If Sharon's plan succeeds, other businesses might adopt similar tactics, potentially harming the market as a whole. This could lead to a loss of consumer trust and damage the industry's overall reputation. A lack of business ethics can have devastating consequences. The Rana Plaza collapse in Bangladesh, where over 1,100 workers died due to disregarded safety standards, is a stark example. Ethics Unwrapped asserts that "firms have an ethical obligation to provide reasonable safety to workers" (Collapse at Rana Plaza, n.d.). Islam & Tahasin (2023) argue that this tragedy resulted from an industry prioritizing profit over ethics, reflecting Bangladesh's struggle with maintaining business ethics. They state, "In addition to considering how their actions would affect shareholders, businesses also need to consider how their decisions will influence other stakeholders, such as consumers, employees, communities, and the environment," emphasizing businesses' responsibility to society at large. Sharon's proposal cannot be considered safe for the industry as a whole.

Impact on Employees

While Sharon's proposal represents a smaller ethical violation compared to the Rana Plaza disaster, it still poses a risk to employee reputation. Employees act as representatives of the company and embody its ethical values. Although Sharon should protect the reputation of her employees and their families, her proposal puts them at risk. Furthermore, unethical work instructions can demotivate employees and negatively impact pizza quality and customer service. Zhao & Qu's (2022) research interestingly found that employees who engage in unethical pro-organizational behavior tend to behave less ethically in their personal lives as well. Employing such individuals poses a risk to both Sharon and the company.

IV. Alternative Solutions

Sharon should present an improvement plan that maintains transparency. For example, she could publicize the blind taste tests conducted during pizza development, demonstrating that they achieved comparable quality to competitors. If Sharon believes customers misunderstand the quality of room service, she should address this misunderstanding with sufficient information. Johns (2023) argues that to clear up misconceptions and to make the customers informed decision, one needs to educate the customer with sufficient information. If confident in the product, she could simply state that they have achieved comparable quality without resorting to exaggerated advertising. Actively seeking and publishing customer feedback can also build trust. All of these strategies demonstrate honesty to consumers by disclosing information and enhancing transparency.
Another approach is to emphasize the faster delivery times of room service due to the proximity of the kitchen. Furthermore, integrating the restaurant's branding with room service could further enhance customer trust.

V. George's Response and Conclusion

George should acknowledge Sharon's efforts, analysis, and the positive aspects of her proposal, but recommend the alternative solutions due to ethical concerns. He needs to convey that a company's credibility is built upon customer trust, and a false brand image can damage the whole reputation. As the restaurant and food service manager, Sharon is expected to uphold high business ethics and prioritize transparency. This situation presents an opportunity for George to enhance the entire company's ethical awareness. Business ethics is complex and constantly evolving. A tagline accepted a year ago might be condemned as discriminatory today. George should consider implementing regular ethics training for Sharon and other employees. This will raise the company's ethical awareness and inform future decision-making.

Conclusion

Business ethics is crucial for business sustainability. While Sharon's proposal is appealing from a profit-driven perspective, it poses significant risks to the company. Ethical considerations are key to recognizing these risks. Companies lacking an ethical compass often pursue short-term gains at the expense of long-term profitability. Long-term success is driven by ethical awareness and the knowledge and experience to act accordingly. Balancing ethics and action is a continuous process. By consistently considering ethical implications in daily decisions, both the company and its employees can develop their ethical understanding. If George and Sharon can reaffirm ethical principles and learn from this situation, their company will be better positioned for sustainable business practices.
