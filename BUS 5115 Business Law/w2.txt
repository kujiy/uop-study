Instructions for Case Analyses
Case Analyses allow students to apply concepts from the course to business situations. Theories, vocabulary, and examples from the readings should be used. Clear and concise writing shows an ability to directly relate learned material to the Cases. Headings and subheadings should be used to delineate which issue and category is being addressed. Ethical Issues –
(A) State the ethical problem(s) or dilemma(s)
(B) Demonstrate a comprehension of ethical concepts. (C) Describe the ethical aspects of the Case.
Identifying Stakeholders –
There are always a set of “usual suspects” in a Case, so be sure to list everyone who might be connected. Remember that business decisions have far reaching effects, so consider anyone who might be affected by the ethical considerations of the Case.
Possible Alternatives –
(A) Identify solutions can be described for each ethical issue identified.
(B) List all solutions and approaches to identified ethical issues.
(C) Alternatives may be drawn from the Case, Readings, or personal experience including outside readings
Ethics of the Alternatives –
(A) Critically assess the alternatives identified for possible recommendation. (B) Describe the ethical implications of each alternative.
(C) Consider external and internal aspects of ethical proposals.
(D) Apply the “utilitarian” perspective (costs and benefits).
1. Which of the alternatives would provide the greatest benefit to the greatest number?
2. What are the costs in each of the alternatives?

Practical Constraints –
(A) Possible obstacles to the implementation of the identified alternatives. (B) Ethical problems that may come up in executing each alternative.
(C) What unwanted outcomes may result from each alternative?
(D) What aspects of the market might obstruct each alternative?
Specific Action –
(A) Make a recommendation
(B) Analysis should provide context for whatever is recommended. (C) Support recommendations with the readings or other literature.
Writing Skill –
Students, teachers, employees, managers, and even corporations are judged by their ability to communicate. Sadly, communication is somewhat dependent upon the form of presentation, and while it is unfair to penalize a writer for not having a native facility with the language they are using, it is fair to reward a writer for presenting ideas in an organized, and understandable fashion. Again, use obvious headings and subheadings. Remember, less is more. Executive Summaries present items summarily. Legal briefs are called “brief” for a reason. Concise and eloquent (not prosaic or elaborate) writing will be rewarded.
Structural Requirements
Using outside resources is encouraged and additional readings round out a student’s understanding of the issues. Use the APA style to identify References used in the analysis. All ideas come from somewhere, so it is fair to ask writers to disclose their sources.
Extra Credit
Thinking outside of the box in this context should be rewarded, not penalized. Don't be afraid to exercise the imagination, and share a unique vision of the Case.

Professional Ethics for Auditors
The Attestation Standards (AS) issued by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) states that auditors are required to exercise professional skepticism to exercise due care in their profession. Jennifer's skepticism is consistent with this standard and is justified by professional ethics.AS 2405 Illegal acts by clients states that if the auditor becomes aware of information regarding possible illegal acts, the auditor should obtain sufficient other information sufficient to assess the nature of the conduct and its impact on the financial statements. Jennifer should conduct a more in-depth investigation to gather facts.
Joseph J. Tapajana, CPA, states that if a CPA suspects fraud by a client, the CPA should alert the client of the concern and make an effort to correct the problem. Importantly, however, Jennifer is not obligated to file charges under either SOX or AICPA standards; both AS 2405 and Tapajana state that a CPA should consider whether to sever ties with the client if the client refuses to take appropriate action. The CPA must consider whether to sever its relationship with the client if the client refuses to take appropriate action. For example, if a CPA discovers an error in a document previously submitted to the taxing authority, the CPA is required to notify the client of the consequences of not correcting the error, but is not required to notify the taxing authority if the client chooses not to correct the error. Thus, the AICPA recognizes that it is generally not permitted to disclose confidential client information without the client's consent. On the other hand, however, Tapajana also states that disclosure without consent may be appropriate where a violation of criminal law has occurred. In any event, Jennifer suspects misconduct by Fantastic Developments and must take action to resolve this issue. The first step is to gather additional information to determine the seriousness of the matter.

Auditor's Responsibility
AS 2405 clearly outlines the auditor's responsibilities in these situations. In summary, it states that it is not the auditor's responsibility to disclose illegal activity to parties other than the client, and that such disclosure would violate the auditor's ethical or legal duty of confidentiality. On the other hand, however, it also states that there may be an obligation to notify non-client parties if that fact would have a material effect on the financial statements. In particular, if the successor auditor has made inquiries in accordance with AS 2610, Initial Audit - Communications Between Predecessor and Successor Auditors, it is permissible to disclose the facts to the successor auditor. Jennifer must, at a minimum, fulfill this responsibility.

Actions Jennifer should take
Because of the complexity of the conflict between the auditor's ethical and legal confidentiality obligations, AS 2405 suggests that the auditor consult with legal counsel before discussing illegal activity with parties outside the client. ジョセフ・J・タパジャナ公認会計士はCPA がクライアントの不正行為を疑った場合、CPA はクライアントに懸念を警告し、問題を修正する努力をするべきだと述べています。しかし重要な点は、SOX法においてもAICPAの基準においても、ジェニファーには告発をする義務はないということです。AS 2405もタパジャナも、クライアントが適切な措置を取ることを拒否した場合はCPA はクライアントとの関係を断つかどうかを検討する必要があるとしています。例えば、CPA が税務当局に以前提出した文書に誤りを発見した場合、CPA は誤りを訂正しない場合の結果をクライアントに通知する必要があるが、クライアントが誤りを訂正しないことを選択した場合は税務当局に通知する必要はないと規定しています。このように、AICPAはクライアントの同意なしに機密クライアント情報を開示することは一般に認められていないことを認識しています。しかし一方で、タパジャナは刑法違反が発生した場合、同意なしに開示することが適切な場合もあるとも述べています。いずれにしても、ジェニファーはファンタスティック デベロップメント社の不正行為を疑っているため、この問題を解決するために行動を起こす必要があります。その第一歩は重大性を判定するための追加情報を収集することです。

監査人の責任
AS2405は、このような状況での監査人の責任をはっきりと示しています。要約すると、クライアント以外の関係者に違法行為を開示することは監査人の責任ではなく、またそのような開示は、監査人の倫理的または法的守秘義務に違反すると述べられています。しかし一方で、その事実が財務諸表に重大な影響を及ぼすのであれば、クライアント以外の関係者に通知する義務が存在する可能性があるとも述べています。特に後任監査人がAS 2610 「初回監査-前任監査人と後任監査人の間のコミュニケーション」に従って問い合わせを行った場合は後任監査人へ事実を開示することが認められています。これはジェニファーにとって大きな希望となるでしょう。
監査人には告発の義務はありませんが、適切な監査をすることは義務となります。AS2405は、財務諸表に法律や規則の違反または違反の可能性が存在しないことについて、経営者から書面による説明を取得することを監査人に求めています。ジェニファーは少なくとも、この責任は果たさなければなりません。

ジェニファーが取るべきアクション
AS2405は監査人の倫理的および法的守秘義務との衝突は複雑であるため、監査人はクライアント外部の当事者と違法行為について話し合う前に法律顧問に相談することを提案しています。ジェニファーは追加の情報を収集し、会計事務所の法律顧問と相談することで、違法行為の重大性を判断するための適切な措置を講じることができます。倫理的にはすぐに告発をするべきだと感じるが、職業倫理、法的責任、および倫理的責任を考慮すると、これまで述べたように追加の情報を収集し、法律顧問と相談することが最善の選択肢です。Fantastic developmentへの疑念を持つことは重要ですが、法的な問題を避けるためには慎重に行動する必要があります。


Conclusion
この紙では、ジェニファーが所属する会計事務所の監査チームが直面している倫理的ジレンマに焦点を当てました。倫理的意思決定のスキームに則って、ジェニファーが取る可能性のある行動とその結果を比較して議論しました。さらに、AICPAのASに則って倫理的および法的行動の重要性について説明し、ジェニファーが直面している選択肢に対する倫理的および法的に適切な選択を特定しました。ジェニファーは、追加の情報を収集し、法律顧問と相談することで、違法行為の重大性を判断するための適切な措置を講じることができます。職業倫理及び業界基準に則って、ジェニファーはこの問題を見過ごさず、適切な行動を起こすことが求められます。
